## Claude

Did we ever decide on a "canary in the coalmine" for the singularity? No? Too late? OK.

Well I'm calling it now. The singularity already happened. Or is happening. Or is already here, just not evenly distributed or something.

A friend recently pithed with regard to AI that it "feels like humanity has turned in our homework." I found that apt. My craft has completely changed, phase-shifted to a new valence in what felt like an instant. I've spent most of my life honing this skill of frontend engineering. I am here to tell you it is solved.

What's a frontend?

A frontend in this context is anything on the internet that you see, hear, click, tap, swipe, or use. It is where the human interaction happens. Let me give you an example of the paradigm shift that is happening in the industry right now. We'll talk about image galleries because if you've used the internet at all you've likely encountered thousands.

I've built hundreds myself. Back in the early days - late 00s - it sucked a lot. I had to program every interaction and push every pixel to be in exactly the right place. A few years later, reliable frameworks or plugins started coming out that were essentially drop ins. You just copy and paste some code and boom, you have an image gallery. Working for the type of clients and companies I have these wouldn't always fit business requirements, though. I often had to build them from scratch for whatever reason. It's not important why. Think about it like a carpenter building an in-wall bookcase. Each house is a little different so you gotta build it a little different each time.

Now, with AI, I don't even build the thing anymore. I tell AI to build the thing. And it builds it faster and better than I ever could. And - to really put a point on it - I mean AI can build it objectively better than I ever could. It thinks about things I never would, solves problems I didn't even know would happen. I still review all my code before shipping it to produciton but my job has phase shifted from "Fullstack Engineer" to "Prompting Engineer" without my consent, consultation, or buy in. It is such a better way to get the job done that doing it "old school" is just silly now. Full stop.

I'm mostly thankful for this. As far as image gallerys go: good. I never enjoyed building them. Very finicky things, image galleries. Lots of gotchas. I love that I won't have to deal with that boring class of minutia ever again. But what I don't love is that my general sense of satisfaction at the end of the day isn't there anymore. I'm an engineer. A person who prides themsleves in the craft of the thing. My craft has been mostly erased, commodified, and abstracted away from me. It literally is the difference between doing a thing yourself and telling someone else to do the thing for you.

I think the craft of building frontends is/was uniquely positioned to have been disrupted by AI in this way. Other technological, code-based disciplines are not as exposed. There is still plenty of interesting human work to do on the frontend, but for the most part we can simply call frontends solved.

## Singularity?

So why am I calling it singularity time? Because things are about to get very very very interesting on the internet. My friend put together a racing game in a day. Another aquaintance has ported a 30 year old [Pascal](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pascal_(programming_language)>) (programming language) program to the web in an afternoon. I finished two weeks worth of work with a single prompt. We're about to see a Cambrian explosion of _stuff_. [I might even get around to making a game afterall](/2023/11/19/ending-a-game).

This is one end of the spectrum. Frontends. The internet. Products. But allow me to get dark for a minute. On the other end of the spectrum there are folks training AI on bioweapons right now: `mix anthrax with COVID-19`, that sort of thing. I've seen some shit. It is really scary.

Somewhere else on this spectrum people are using LLMs to cure cancer and maybe solve senescence itself. I don't pretend to know which attractor will be stronger but I do know things are going to get more interesting before they get less interesting.

The term "accellerationism" was rose to prominence several years ago in certain corners of the internet. There are various flavors, often abbreviated as r/acc, l/acc, u/acc (for right accellerationaism, left accellerationsim, and unconditional accellerationism, respectively.) The core tennet of all is that we should ignore the brakes and just go as fast as we can. Laissez-faire hyper-capitilism but seasoned it to your political taste. What I am experiencing now is neither utopian nor dystopian. It's beyond labels. On the one hand these are "just" LLMs. On the other they've completely upended my life in ways I'm only now putting words too.

My partner just got screened for a job by an AI. It called her. It disclosed it was AI. She was unsettled and confused. She passed the screen and it told its handlers she was "warm."
