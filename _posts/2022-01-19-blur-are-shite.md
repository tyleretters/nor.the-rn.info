>Hope is an orientation towards the future, but I worry that trying to think about hope in this way makes it serious -- po-faced and a rather somber affair. But this runs counter to real-life experience - some of the times that we might feel that sense of the future opening up, of possibility, of that anticipatory consciousness, are moments of joy, of laughter, and of celebration. All of this happens in bleak, dark times too -- sometimes laughter<!--x--> is most necessary when the world is at its worst if only to establish, for a brief moment, the impermanence of the horrors around us.
