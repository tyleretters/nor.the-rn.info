## Childhood

Tyler Etters was born in a hospital surrounded by the frozen corn fields of northern Illinois. It was January, 1988. His mother's words when she first held him were, "Wow, he has an old soul."

He enjoyed a "free range" childhood: playing with his friends in the endless forest preserves, wetlands, and fields of the region. Come home by dark. Don't pick the wildflowers. Stay away from strangers. When he wasn't playing outside, Tyler could be found building robots, space ships, and sprawling cities with Legos.

Tyler's parents divorced when he was two. Despite this, both parents would remain present and active throughout his life. With dad it was camping trips to Devil's Lake, pitch-perfect re-enactment of the *Christmas Story* BB gun gift scene, fishing for bluegills. With mom, summer adventures to Door County, watching Star Trek (TNG & Voyager) and The X-Files on Sunday nights, tagging along on prairie burns.

One time Tyler and his mother were home during a tornado. They were lying in bed and looking through the skylight. The clouds were green and pink somehow. The wind picked up. Suddenly: a cacophonous crashing noise and the skylight completely obscured. An oak tree had snapped in the wind and crashed into their little house. They were unharmed.

In third grade, Tyler was accepted into the Program for Academically Talented Students (PATS). During math and reading hours, a small cadre of PATS kids would convene from various classrooms and learn from an accelerated curriculum together. These classes would set Tyler on a trajectory of curiosity. He became fascinated by mathematics, language, and outer space.

One year, he was given an electric guitar as a gift. With his mom's permission and oversight, he pawned it to buy a desktop computer.

Tyler's sibling, Willow, was born in 1995. With a seven-year age delta, they found themselves with a narrow band of activities to share. Usually, Willow was perfectly happy just watching Tyler play games like Starcraft or Metal Gear Solid. They also enjoyed catching snakes together.

In middle school Tyler spent many study halls and geometry classes teaching himself programming on his TI-83 Plus graphic calculator. Using TI-BASIC, he created text-based adventure games with mountains of `goto` statements and random number generators. He played Magic: The Gathering, Doom, Warhammer 40,000, and spent a lot of time with friends who were also total nerds.
