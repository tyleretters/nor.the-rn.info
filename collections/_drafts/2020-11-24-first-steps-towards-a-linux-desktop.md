It is time. I'm going keeping this log of my process for posterity, future installs, and personal enrichment.

## Research

Like all intentional projects, I begin with research. My dear friend, [@argotechnica](https://twitter.com/argotechnica)'s recommendation of Ubuntu LTS 20.04 almost had me skip the research phase altogether. I trust him absolutely in matters such as these, you see. I went about partitioning disk space and creating a flash drive, but something felt off. Indeed, when I tried the install, I was greeted with an indecipherable `nouveau` error of some-sort.

![nouveau Errors](/assets/images/linux-errors.jpg)

I typically have a high "sync rate" with new technologies. Things sorta just work the first time through. This was an interesting portent and gave me pause - I hadn't even gotten into the complex stuff yet.

I shelved the project and gave myself some time to ruminate.

---

Circadian rhythm decimated by the ravages of 2020, I found myself wide awake at 03:30 researching [Endeavor OS](https://endeavouros.com/). I think this is the one.

Of course, I've consulted the tables and charts and blogs and think-pieces and comparisons between the different distros. Of particular interest to me is the art of ricing. Long had I been an appreciative lurker of the Unix Tumblrs. Finally, computer interfaces that look like the computer interfaces 20th century sci-fi promised us.

There appears to be a camp of thought that sees ricing as a superfluous exercise in form over function. The scoff at it. As a designer, I could not be more offended. Design is intelligence made visible. How a thing looks is only part of how it works. When designing a good interface you'll often get the looks "for free." In other words, you need to pick a color so why not pick a color you like? And why not pick a color that has an optical relationship with already chosen colors? And why not pick a color that has semantic/syntactic meaning?

No, ricing is not an exercise in personal expression or vanity. It is the apotheosis of interface design.

---

I'm skeptical of the USB drive I used to install Ubuntu - the only USB drive I own. I ordered a new one.
