# The Capitalist Nihilism of the Alien Franchise

If sacrificing a child for personal monetary gain is not an example of capitalist nihilism, I don't know what is. This is exactly what XXX, YYY's character in Aliens (19XX), attempts to do when he locks Ripley and Newt in the medbay with a facehugger. He'll return to the Company with their impregnated bodies and receive a fat bonus check (or so he thinks). He does wrestle with the morality of this a bit, but at the end of the day the promise of fortune wins out.

Foremost, I read the the Alien films through a feminist, pro-choice lense. Much has been written about the strength of Ripley, the non-assignment of genders in the original script, the medical pod that "is not configured" for women.

In the opening scene of Alien, we learn that the Nostromo is a salvage ship. Let us take a moment to consider how staggering of a departure from the established norms this is, as well as the etymology of the Nostromo itself.

Star Trek is about boldly going where none have. Star Wars is about the mythic struggle of light versus dark. Dune is about transcendence. Bladerunner is about humanity.

Even though the "salvage" ship may have originally been a clever writing solution to give way to the conceits that follow it could have just as easily been a scientific expedition. The characters motives are strictly financial. This is their job, and none are particularly passionate about it! ("Mommy, I want to grow up and work on a salvage ship!")



On what, or whom, do these characters spend their money on? In Alien 3, XXX, says, "I have a wife and kids." This is absurd. Between years of hypersleep and however many decades on the FuryXXX are we really to take his statement for truth?

The closest glimpse to the "normal" world we get may be in Aliens, aboard the XXX. This ship appears to have some semblance of a megamall...

The breif open scene (and childhood flashbacks) in Prometheous are so out of place they can perhaps be dismissed. The archelogists, appearing to have just come back from company sponsored trip to REI, are happily doing their work among *green* mountains and *breathable* air. Notably, this is perhaps the only scene in the entire franchise where individual expression via clothing (unique colors, different styles) appears. Everywhere else it is standard issue fatigues and uniforms.

No, I've always imagined earth or "home" to be even worse than than whatever's out there.

Despite our various storytellers over the years cherry picking the least pleasant excursions for our viewing pleasure, it would appear as though the colonialism of Weyland-Yutani is a staggering success. To thing the megastructures of FuryXXX and AliensPLANET were just plopped down on orbiting backwater stars...

So again, what exactly do they spend it on? It has long been posited that Bladerunner exists in the same universe as Alien. One of the first replicants appaer to have two primary functions: manual labor and sex work. Do people spend their time in company sponsored brothels with company made droids on company designed barbituates?
